What it is

A real instrument.
Not a quiz.

This is a full HEXACO personality assessment — 120 scored items across six broad domains and twenty-four underlying facets, with your results placed against a normative sample so you can see where you actually sit, not just what your raw answers were.

HEXACO is one of the more rigorous trait models in personality science — it captures what trait-based profiling reliably can: how you tend to think, feel, defend, pursue, avoid, and relate, across consistent and well-studied dimensions. It does not diagnose you, type you, or sort you into a category. It maps tendencies, not a verdict.

You'll get a plain-language overview of your results — not just numbers, but what those numbers tend to mean in how a person actually lives, where the strengths in a pattern tend to double as costs, and where the same trait shows up differently depending on what it's pointed at.

It's free, genuinely anonymous, and there's no login or signup. Nothing is stored on our side. You can download your results as a PDF or raw data at the end.

A note on the instrument itself: this tool is built on the open International Personality Item Pool (IPIP) HEXACO item set — a validated proxy for the HEXACO model, not the official, copyrighted HEXACO-PI-R instrument. Your percentile scores are calculated against provisional norms from a large anonymous online dataset, not a clinical or nationally representative sample. This is a free, non-commercial, exploratory tool — useful for self-reflection and coaching preparation, not a clinical, diagnostic, or selection instrument.

What it measures

Six domains.
Twenty-four facets.

Honesty-Humility

How you relate to status, advantage, and special treatment — especially when there's something to gain and no one's watching.

Emotionality

Sensitivity to threat, loss, and vulnerability — the volume of the internal signal that says this matters, don't ignore it.

Extraversion

How much energy and reward you draw from engagement with the world — people, activity, visibility, momentum.

Agreeableness

What happens when friction shows up: patience or irritation, accommodation or resistance, letting go or holding on.

Conscientiousness

How tightly behaviour is organised around structure, standards, effort, and delayed reward.

Openness to Experience

Appetite for novelty, complexity, ambiguity, and ways of seeing that aren't the obvious or familiar ones.

What it isn't

Useful.
Not enough.

This is an outside view — a structured snapshot of patterns in how you tend to think, react, relate, avoid, and pursue. It is genuinely useful as that. It is not, and cannot be, the whole picture.

It doesn't show how these patterns actually play out in the specific conditions of your life. It doesn't show what maintains them, where they protect you, where they cost you, or how to work with them in real time. That work can't be done from the outside, and a report — however accurate — can't do it for you.

If something in your results feels accurate, uncomfortable, or hard to dismiss, that's usually where the real work begins, not where it ends.

Where this fits

The map,
not the path.

Profiling is one of three things this work runs on, alongside direct mindfulness training and applied behavioural work. The profile gives a working hypothesis for where the real friction is and where the real leverage is — not as a diagnosis, but as a starting hypothesis worth testing against an actual life.

In one-to-one work, a profile like this is the beginning of the conversation, not the end of it. It gets tested against your actual relationships, decisions, avoidance patterns, and the places you already half-know something isn't working — until a vague pattern becomes something specific enough to actually train.
